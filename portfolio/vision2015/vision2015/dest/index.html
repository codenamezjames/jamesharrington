• In 2009, in the midst of a national recession and unprecedented unemployment, the Greater Pensacola Chamber conceived of a five-year plan for local job creation. It was a groundbreaking initiative designed to leverage the combined power of local stakeholders—small businesses, large corporations, government entities, civic organizations and individuals--to drive positive economic change in the Pensacola Bay area. Some believed the concept to be overambitious in wake of Pensacola’s recent population declines, joblessness and general community malaise. Undeterred, the partnership forged ahead, and Vision 2015 officially launched in January 2010. Its goals were lofty: raise $6.5M, create 3,000 new jobs and do it all in five years.

    • Growth & Expansion

      Information Technology

      The Vision 2015 initiative sought to bring high paying jobs to Pensacola’s economic section from various industries. To date, more than 450 new information technology jobs have been added to our local community including positions with innovative new start-ups.

      Financial Services

      Financial service providers committed more than 7,000 jobs to the Vision 2015 campaign, making it the largest industry supporting job growth in Pensacola. These new jobs provide high-earning opportunities and support millions of dollars annually to our local economy.

      Manufacturing

      More than 140 new manufacturing jobs were committed from various companies across the city. The manufacturing section is a cornerstone of innovation in our local and global economies. These jobs traditionally pay well, provide benefits and opportunities for personal growth.
